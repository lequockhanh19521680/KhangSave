TERMINAL REPLACEMENT

These steps must be followed when replacing a terminal.
If the wire harness is protected with CFWs, refer to [Extra].

Step 1. Identify the connector and the terminal type.

Step 2. Disconnect the terminal from the connector.

Step 3. Replace the terminal.

Step 4. Install the terminal into the connector.




Extra. Special handling for the wires that are protected with CFWs.

Step 1. Identify the connector and the terminal type.

1. Connector Body

  1. Identify the part number of the corresponding terminal with wire according to the above-mentioned 1.

  1. Identify the part number of the connector body by picking out the correct one from the illustrations according to the number of terminals and the shape.


REFERENCE
(a) If you can find no connector which matches the type you require, pick out male and female connector bodies as a set which have more terminals than you require.
For the combined terminal type connector body, pick out and appropriate connector body for each terminal being used.

Example
  • When two types of terminal are used in one connector body, pick out a male and female for two different connector bodies.
(b) The part number has been stamped on the back of the connector body. (This will continue to be done to new types of connector.)


2. Terminal with Wire

Identify the part number of the terminal with wire by picking out the corresponding illustration from the pictures provided on the following charts.

REFERENCE
  • In some of the illustrations, there are two part numbers for the same illustration of terminal with wire, because although the shapes of the terminals are completely the same, there is a difference in with /without gold-plating (gilded).

NOTICE
  • When the terminal used is gold plated, be sure to replace it with a gold plated terminal when marking repairs.
  • Do not use male and female terminals which are made of different materials from each other.
