Extra. Special handling for the wires that are protected with CFWs.

1. When a terminal or multiple terminals are replaced:

If wires are protected with CFWs and the extra wire length shown in the illustration is shorter than 18 mm (A), use the following procedure for proper repair.

NOTICE:
The procedures instructed below are supplements to the original terminal replacement procedure. To replace terminals, refer to the original procedure from steps 1 to 4 as well.

  1. Cut the applicable wire(s) at somewhere around A.

  2. Cut the applicable wire(s) at somewhere around B, and then remove the terminal(s) from the connector housing.

HINT:
Do not remove the cut wires from the CFW so as to prevent other wires from being damaged.

  1. Prepare for new repair wire(s) with length of 500 mm.

  1. If multiple terminals need to be replaced, change the cut points of the new wires as shown in the illustration.

  1. After the terminal(s) is replaced, bind the wire(s) of the terminal(s) to the CFW protector(s) by wrapping silicon tape around them.

2. When all the terminals need to be replaced, or if CFWs protectors have been broken:

NOTICE:
The procedures instructed below are supplements to the original terminal replacement procedure. To replace terminals, refer to the original procedure from steps 1 to 4 as well.

  1. Cut all the wires at somewhere around A, and remove all the terminals from the connector housing.

  1. Prepare for new repair wires with length of 500 mm.

  1. Change the cut points of the new wires as shown in the illustration.

  1. After all the terminals are inserted into the connector housing, wrap a convoluted tube or similar, instead of CFWs, around the wires to bind them together.


  2. If wire harness clamps had been used, install new clamps onto the convoluted tube.
