Step 3. Replace the terminal.

Copper wires

1. Select the appropriate repair wire terminal

  1. Select the correct replacement terminal with lead, from the supply parts.




2. Replace the wire terminal.

  1. Use the new wire lead as a guide for proper length.

REFERENCE
  • If the length of wire removed is not approximately the same length as the new piece, the following problems may develop:

  • Too short -- tension on the terminal, splice, or the connector, causing and open circuit.
  • Too long -- excessive wire near the connector, may get pinched or abraded, causing a short circuit.


REFERENCE
  • When connecting a wire harness at there or more spots to the same connector, cut the wire harness as shown on the right.


3. Remove the sheaths from the existing wire and the repair terminal wire.

Start stripping at least 8 mm to 11 mm away from the end of the existing harness at vehicle side and also from the end of the repair wire.


A(mm) 8~11


NOTICE
Do not damage or cut the wire strand. After the sheath is removed, visually check the wire. If any damage is found, do the sheath removal again.


4. Select Correct Size of Sleeve from the Supply Parts.

  1. Using a caliper, measure the outer diameter of the wire or the diameter of the wire strand.

    • When the sleeve size is determined according to the wire outer diameter:
      Measure the outer diameter of the wire using a caliper.

    • When the sleeve size is determined according to the nominal diameter of the wire strand:
      Measure the diameter of the wire strand using a caliper.

5. Join the strands of the existing wire and new terminal wire

NOTICE
If unauthorized work procedures, crimping tools or sleeves are used in copper wire repair, an improper wire connection may occur.

  1. When nominal size is 0.3 or more
    Overlap the two stripped wire ends inside the sleeve as illustrated on the right.



REFERENCE
  • You might find it easier if you use a miniature special tool as a guide as you insert wires into the sleeve.


  1. When nominal size is less than 0.3.

    • Two stripped wire ends are arranged for the same as illustrated on the right.

    • Twist the two stripped wire ends together.

    • Insert the twisted wire ends into the sleeve.


  1. The crimp portion of the copper wire crimping tool has the color marks on it. Place the sleeve in the correct portion of the color mark that corresponds to the color of the sleeve.



  1. With the center of the sleeve correctly placed between the crimping jaws, squeeze the crimping tool until either end comes into contact at the section marked by "CLOSE HERE".

REFERENCE
  • Check to see that the sleeve and wires are still in the correct position before closing the crimping tool ends with steady pressure.


  1. Pull the joined wires to either end. Make sure that they are joined firmly by the sleeve.

NOTICE
If the connected wire(s) is pulled out from the crimped sleeve or crimping has failed, replace the sleeve with a new one and then crimp again.


  1. Crimp both ends of the sleeve with the crimping tool at the "INS" position.



6. Waterproof and insulate the joint area

Wrap silicon tape around the joins to protect them from moisture.

REFERENCE
  • This job is required in repairs of the engine compartment, under the floor and other moisture entry positions.


REFERENCE
  • Before starting the operation, thoroughly wipe dirt and grease off the sections to be joined.
  • If the adhesive surfaces of two tapes come in contact they will stick together and will not come apart, so do not remove the backing film except when using the tape.
  • Do not let oil and dust, etc. get on the tape surface.

  1. Ready about 100 mm (3.94 in.) of silicon tape (Part No. 08231-00045) and peel off the film.

  1. Stretch the silicon tape until its width is reduced by half.

  1. About 10 mm (0.39 in.) from the end of the sleeve, wrap the silicon tape around the sleeve three or more times while stretching the tape.

  1. Wrap the remaining part of the sleeve with half of the tape overlapping at each turn.



  1. Firmly wrap the tape two times or more about 10 mm (0.39 in.) from the other end of the sleeve, then wrap the tape back towards the start again and firmly finish winding the tape around the center of the sleeve.
